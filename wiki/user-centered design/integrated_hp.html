April 7th, 2021

Dr. Tracy Kion

Dr. Kion said TNFa could flow into the TME as TNFa is present in the body already. However, because we are using an AND-gate, our detection method is credible. She also suggested we have different sensors in different strains as this would allow for more biosensors and scoring.

She gave insight into our understanding of the Sip A promoter for our construct. She said that using SipA as our promoter might work. However she said that although SipA might be upregulated, it might not be the regulator. She also gave advice on using Salmonella for our experiment. She said that Salmonella follows the normal growth curve and does well in nutrient deficient environments. She also suggested an experimental design to test for endotoxins for our attenuated strain. This is important because endotoxins can be harmful for the body , so this would limit the applications of our project. She suggested some troubleshooting steps for the colony PCR. She thinks that it would be helpful to visualize Salmonella gene segments using Snapgene. She said that cloning operons can be difficult as they have one promoter so she suggested we clone a big chunk of DNA upstream of sicA. Lastly, she recommended we insert our pieces into a chromosome if plasmid insertions do not work. .

April 15th, 2021

Dr. Saltzman (Salspera)

He reminded us of how important our work is by recognizing that it has potential to help clinicians perform cancer diagnosis and treatment with enhanced efficiency. While he acknowledged that it is unlikely this project could replace biopsies entirely, he mentioned that it could serve as a great complementary tool. He also noted that our tool may actually be more sensitive to tissue localization and movement than traditional radiology procedures. One potential application of our project he could foresee was in aiding clinicians with localization/identification of tumour areas during pre-operative planning, and possibly during surgical/radiology procedures as well. In advancing our project further, he noted that we may want to determine the advantages/disadvantages of delivering the attenuated salmonella orally or intravenously. He also suggested we look into performing in vivo experimentation with subcutaneously implanted tumours in mice to determine clinical efficacy further.

May 13th, 2021

Dr. Kevin Bennewith

He thinks the rationale for our project is sound, and suggested some more things to address in our project pitches going forward. For what we can produce to modulate Tumour Microenvironment (TME), he said MMP inhibitors can make things worse, and no single cytokine recruits only anti-tumour immune cells. We could look into collagenases, compounds to increase blood perfusion or modulate vasculature to decrease hypoxia and increase immune cell infiltration.

For 3D TME modeling, he suggested many aspects we could try to model vasculature + gradients of hypoxia and necrosis surrounding vasculature, fluid exchange, pH, the localization of different immune cell populations within the tumour, cancer associated fibroblasts. He thinks modulation of macrophage phenotype could be promising to explore (the LPS and immunogenic proteins in Salmonella may already help skew towards M1) For cancer spheroids, would take about 2 weeks to grow a pea-sized one with the right cell lines. He suggests for every experiment we design, keep the research question focused and firmly in mind.

July 16th, 2021

Dr. Anne-Marie Fortier (Park Lab)

To create the hydrogel model, we would need cancer cells (ex. MDA 231 breast cancer cells), immune cells (Jurkat), and fibroblasts (ex. fetal IMR 90).

We could use a 96 well plate + grow the cells over a week or two Mix cancer cells with fibroblasts in matrigel, then add immune cells with media on top and they will migrate down by gravity, need to put cytokines in the matrigel to attract immune cells to that layer (CXCR family) Can use that as a positive + control; in this condition, the immune cells can migrate towards the cytokines.

Suggested we use IFN gamma and granzyme B (using imaging) as a biomarker; if granzyme B is present, then immune cells are in "killing mode" + state of T cells can be checked by flow cytometry She gave suggestions on creating a model for hot tumours and cold tumours. 3D modelling has some advantages over mouse models: less expensive, and possible to test different drug treatments simultaneously
